Overview map of crime and incarceration in USA during 2016

Based on the map of the United States provided, we can observe distinct trends in crime distribution across different states.

Crime Rates in the Northwestern Region: This indicates that the frequency of criminal activities in the northwestern part of the United States is comparatively lower than in other regions. This may also reflect the effectiveness of social safety measures and law enforcement agencies in this area.

Moderate Crime Rates in the Eastern Region: This suggests that the eastern region experiences a moderate level of criminal activity. Complex social, economic, and geographical factors may be at play. For instance, the presence of large cities might lead to a certain level of criminal activity, but at the same time, there may be more law enforcement agencies and resources available for control and prevention.

High Crime Rates in the Southern Region: This signifies that the southern region experiences a higher frequency of criminal activities compared to other areas. There may be a range of social issues at play, such as economic instability, high unemployment rates, lower educational levels, and social conflicts. This might require more resources and efforts to address the issue of crime.

The population of each state VS the number of crimes in each state

According to the scatterplot plot on the left, we can observe a strong correlation between the number of residents committing crimes and the overall population in the 50 states. The scatterplot illustrates that states with higher population densities tend to have a greater number of residents who engage in criminal activities. Florida, in particular, exhibits an interesting trend: as the years progress, the number of crimes in the state increases in tandem with its growing population.
